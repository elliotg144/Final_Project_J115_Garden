How to Repair Your Garden After a Tropical Storm

One Big Cycle

Tying it Together

After Tropical Storm Isaias came through the Eastern side of the United States, gardens throughout the area were severely displaced. These storms often come come quick leave blown down trees, split stalks, and scattered debris in their path. In order to repair, gardeners have introduced jute, stakes, and a whole lot of out-of-the-box thinking to bring things close to the way they were.

Jute is a common type of twine used to reattach and and hold together split stalks. It's comprised of hemp fibers which can withstand high rates of stress while maintaining ultimate flexibility. A wide variety of knots are used to sinch together damaged plants.

Marty's been through enough storms to learn how to appreciate them at this point. Although they require more clean-up than he prefers, "ya know, if they didn't always break my stuff, I'd really think they're something beautiful, sort of like fire".

Just like fire, storms offer a soundtrack to go along with their destruction. Looking for a silver lining, "storms offer one of the few soundtracks that never get old".
